About DRC

The Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), also known as Congo-Kinshasa, is the second-largest country in Africa by area and has a population of over 95 million people. It is bordered by nine countries, including Angola, Rwanda, Uganda, and Tanzania.

DRC is rich in natural resources, including minerals like cobalt and copper, and is home to the Congo Rainforest, the second-largest rainforest in the world. The capital city is Kinshasa, which is also the largest city in the country.

The Congo River, the world's second-largest river by discharge, flows through the country, providing opportunities for transportation, fishing, and hydroelectric power.

Key Ministries

Ministry of Interior

Responsible for internal security and public administration.

Ministry of Foreign Affairs

Handles DRC's diplomatic relations and international cooperation.

Ministry of Health

Oversees healthcare policy and public health services.

Ministry of Education

Manages education policy and schools nationwide.

Ministry of Mines

Manages DRC's vast mineral resources, including cobalt and copper.

Tourist Attractions

Virunga National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to mountain gorillas, active volcanoes, and diverse wildlife.

Congo River

The world's second-largest river by discharge, offering opportunities for fishing, transportation, and exploration.

Salonga National Park

The largest tropical rainforest reserve in Africa, home to bonobos and other unique species.

Did You Know?

  • DRC is the second-largest country in Africa by area.
  • The Congo Rainforest is the second-largest rainforest in the world, after the Amazon.
  • DRC is one of the world's largest producers of cobalt, a key component in batteries.
  • French is the official language, with Lingala, Swahili, Kikongo, and Tshiluba as national languages.
