About Kenya

Kenya, officially the Republic of Kenya, is a country in Eastern Africa with a population of approximately 54 million people. It is bordered by Tanzania to the south, Uganda to the west, South Sudan to the northwest, Ethiopia to the north, Somalia to the northeast, and the Indian Ocean to the southeast.

Kenya gained independence from Britain on December 12, 1963, and became a republic in 1964. The capital and largest city is Nairobi, a regional commercial hub. Kenya's economy is the largest in East Africa, with agriculture, tourism, and services being key sectors.

The country is famous for its scenic landscapes and wildlife preserves. The Maasai Mara National Reserve is known for its annual wildebeest migrations, and Amboseli National Park offers views of Tanzania's Mount Kilimanjaro.

Key Ministries

Ministry of Interior

Responsible for internal security, public administration, and national government coordination.

Ministry of Foreign Affairs

Handles Kenya's diplomatic relations and international affairs.

Ministry of Health

Oversees healthcare policy and public health services nationwide.

Ministry of Education

Manages education policy, schools, and higher education institutions.

Ministry of Tourism

Promotes Kenya's tourism industry and wildlife conservation.

Tourist Attractions

Maasai Mara National Reserve

Famous for the Great Migration where over 2 million wildebeest, zebra and gazelle migrate annually. Offers exceptional wildlife viewing.

Amboseli National Park

Known for its large elephant herds and views of Mount Kilimanjaro across the border in Tanzania.

Lamu Old Town

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this is the oldest and best-preserved Swahili settlement in East Africa.

Did You Know?

  • Kenya is home to the world's fastest runners, with Kenyan athletes dominating long-distance running competitions.
  • The Great Rift Valley runs through Kenya, containing some of the world's most important paleoanthropological sites.
  • Kenya has over 40 national parks and reserves, protecting about 8% of its land area.
  • Swahili and English are the official languages, with Swahili being the national language.
