About Rwanda

Rwanda, officially the Republic of Rwanda, is a landlocked country in East Africa with a population of about 13 million people. It is bordered by Uganda to the north, Tanzania to the east, Burundi to the south, and DR Congo to the west.

Known as "the land of a thousand hills," Rwanda is famous for its mountainous terrain and is one of Africa's most densely populated countries. Since the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi, Rwanda has made remarkable progress in economic development, social cohesion, and governance.

Kigali, the capital, is considered one of Africa's cleanest and safest cities. Rwanda has become a leader in environmental protection, banning plastic bags in 2008 and implementing ambitious reforestation programs.

Tourist Attractions

Volcanoes National Park

Home to endangered mountain gorillas, made famous by Dian Fossey's research. Offers incredible gorilla trekking experiences.

Nyungwe Forest National Park

One of Africa's oldest rainforests with 13 primate species including chimpanzees and colobus monkeys. Features canopy walkway.

Kigali Genocide Memorial

Powerful memorial documenting the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi, honoring victims and promoting reconciliation.

Did You Know?

  • Rwanda is the first country in the world with a female majority in parliament (61% as of 2023).
  • The country has a monthly community service day called Umuganda where citizens clean neighborhoods.
  • Rwanda has the highest representation of women in government globally.
  • Kinyarwanda, French, and English are the official languages, with Kinyarwanda spoken by nearly all Rwandans.
