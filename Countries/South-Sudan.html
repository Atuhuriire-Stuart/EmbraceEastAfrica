About South Sudan

South Sudan, officially the Republic of South Sudan, is a landlocked country in East Africa with a population of about 11 million people. It is bordered by Sudan to the north, Ethiopia to the east, Kenya to the southeast, Uganda to the south, DR Congo to the southwest, and the Central African Republic to the west.

South Sudan gained independence from Sudan on July 9, 2011, making it the youngest country in the world. The capital city is Juba. The country is rich in natural resources, particularly oil, which accounts for the majority of its economy.

South Sudan is home to diverse cultures and ethnic groups, with over 60 indigenous languages spoken. The White Nile flows through the country, providing opportunities for agriculture and fishing.

Key Ministries

Ministry of Interior

Responsible for internal security and public administration.

Ministry of Foreign Affairs

Handles South Sudan's diplomatic relations and international cooperation.

Ministry of Health

Oversees healthcare policy and public health services.

Ministry of Education

Manages education policy and schools nationwide.

Ministry of Petroleum

Manages South Sudan's oil resources and energy sector.

Tourist Attractions

The Sudd Wetlands

One of the world's largest wetlands, home to diverse wildlife and bird species.

Nimule National Park

Known for its scenic beauty and wildlife, including elephants and hippos.

White Nile

Offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and exploring the river's ecosystem.

Did You Know?

  • South Sudan is the youngest country in the world, gaining independence in 2011.
  • The Sudd Wetlands are one of the largest freshwater ecosystems in the world.
  • South Sudan is home to over 60 ethnic groups, making it one of Africa's most diverse nations.
  • English is the official language, but many indigenous languages are widely spoken.
