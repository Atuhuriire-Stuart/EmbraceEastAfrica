About Burundi

Burundi, officially the Republic of Burundi, is a landlocked country in East Africa with a population of about 12 million people. It is bordered by Rwanda to the north, Tanzania to the east and south, and DR Congo to the west.

Burundi gained independence from Belgium on July 1, 1962. The capital city is Gitega, while Bujumbura remains the largest city and economic hub. The country is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional drumming.

Burundi's economy is primarily based on agriculture, with coffee and tea being the main exports. The country is also home to Lake Tanganyika, one of the world's deepest and oldest freshwater lakes.

National Anthem

Burundi Bwacu (Our Burundi)

Kirundi Version

Verse 1:
Burundi bwacu, Burundi buhire,
Shinga icumu mu mashinga,
Gaba intahe y'ubugabo ku bugingo.
Warapfunywe ntiwapfuye,
Warahabishijwe ntiwahababuka,
Uhagurukana, uhagurukana, uhagurukana, ubugingo urikukira.

Verse 2:
Shinga icumu mu mashinga,
Shinga icumu mu mashinga,
Burundi bwacu, ragi ry'abasokuru,
Ramutswa intahe n'ibihugu,
Ufatanije ishyaka n'ubumwe,
Uhagurukana, uhagurukana, uhagurukana, ubugingo urikukira.

Verse 3:
Burundi bwacu, nkora mutima kuri twese,
Tugutuye amaboko, umutima n'ubuzima,

English Translation

Verse 1:
Our Burundi, blessed Burundi,
Take your spear, take your shield,
And the strength of your warriors.
You were beaten but not conquered,
You were humiliated but not enslaved,
Stand up, stand up, stand up, the time has come.

Verse 2:
Take your spear, take your shield,
Take your spear, take your shield,
Our Burundi, heritage of our ancestors,
Be worthy of their sacrifices,
United in heart and mind,
Stand up, stand up, stand up, the time has come.

Verse 3:
Our Burundi, we give you our hearts,
We offer you our arms, our hearts and our lives,
To serve you with joy.
Let us all pray together,
May God bless our Burundi,
Stand up, stand up, stand up, the time has come.

Key Ministries

Ministry of Interior

Responsible for internal security and local governance.

Ministry of Foreign Affairs

Handles Burundi's diplomatic relations and international cooperation.

Ministry of Health

Oversees healthcare policy and public health services.

Ministry of Education

Manages education policy and schools nationwide.

Ministry of Environment

Leads conservation efforts and environmental protection.

Tourist Attractions

Lake Tanganyika

One of the world's deepest and oldest freshwater lakes, offering stunning views and biodiversity.

Kibira National Park

A rainforest reserve home to chimpanzees, colobus monkeys, and diverse bird species.

Gitega National Museum

Showcases Burundi's rich cultural heritage, including traditional artifacts and drumming performances.

Did You Know?

  • Burundi is one of the smallest countries in Africa, but it has a rich cultural heritage.
  • Drumming is an integral part of Burundian culture and is recognized by UNESCO as an intangible cultural heritage.
  • Lake Tanganyika is home to over 250 species of fish, many of which are found nowhere else in the world.
  • Kirundi and French are the official languages, with Swahili also widely spoken in urban areas.
