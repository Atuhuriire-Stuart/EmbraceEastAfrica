About Uganda

Uganda, officially the Republic of Uganda, is a landlocked country in East Africa with a population of about 45 million people. It is bordered by South Sudan to the north, Kenya to the east, Tanzania and Rwanda to the south, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the west.

Uganda gained independence from Britain on October 9, 1962. The capital and largest city is Kampala. Uganda's economy has grown significantly in recent decades, with agriculture, services, and oil production being key sectors. The country is known as the "Pearl of Africa" for its natural beauty.

Uganda is home to diverse wildlife including mountain gorillas, chimpanzees, and the Big Five game animals. It contains the source of the Nile River at Jinja and shares Lake Victoria, Africa's largest lake, with Kenya and Tanzania.

English Version

Verse 1:

We lay our future in thy hand;
United, free for liberty
Together we'll always stand.

Verse 2:
Oh Uganda! the land of freedom,

Key Ministries

Ministry of Internal Affairs

Responsible for internal security, immigration, and public administration.

Ministry of Foreign Affairs

Handles Uganda's diplomatic relations and international cooperation.

Ministry of Health

Oversees national healthcare policy and public health services.

Ministry of Education & Sports

Manages education policy and sports development nationwide.

Ministry of Tourism

Promotes Uganda's tourism industry and wildlife conservation.

Tourist Attractions

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

UNESCO World Heritage Site home to nearly half of the world's mountain gorilla population, offering unique gorilla trekking experiences.

Murchison Falls National Park

Uganda's largest national park where the Nile River explodes through a narrow gorge to create the spectacular Murchison Falls.

Rwenzori Mountains

Known as the "Mountains of the Moon," featuring Africa's third highest peak and unique alpine vegetation.

Did You Know?

  • Uganda is home to over half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas.
  • The Nile River, the world's longest river, starts its 6,650km journey in Uganda at Jinja.
  • Uganda has the youngest population in the world, with over 78% of its population under 30 years.
  • English and Swahili are the official languages, with Luganda being widely spoken in the central region.
