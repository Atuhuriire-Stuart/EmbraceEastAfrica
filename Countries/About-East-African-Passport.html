East African Passport

The official travel document for the East African Community

Overview

The East African Passport is a travel document issued by member states of the East African Community (EAC) to their citizens for international travel within the region and beyond. It symbolizes the regional integration and cooperation among EAC partner states.

Seamless Travel

Enjoy visa-free movement across all EAC member states with this passport.

Regional Integration

Demonstrates your citizenship in the East African Community.

International Recognition

Accepted worldwide as a valid travel document.

Member States

Burundi

DR Congo

Kenya

Requirements

To apply for an East African Passport, you must provide:

  • Completed application form
  • National ID or birth certificate
  • Recent passport-sized photographs
  • Proof of citizenship from an EAC member state
  • Payment of applicable fees
  • For renewals: Previous passport

Application Process

Follow these steps to obtain your East African Passport:

Gather Documents

Collect all required documents listed above.

Complete Application

Fill out the application form available at immigration offices or online portals.

Submit Application

Visit your nearest immigration office with all documents and payment.

Biometric Capture

Have your fingerprints and photo taken at the immigration office.

Processing

Wait for processing (typically 7-14 working days).

Collection

Pick up your passport when notified, or opt for delivery if available.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the East African Passport valid?

The standard East African Passport is valid for 10 years for adults and 5 years for minors (under 18 years).

Can I use it for international travel outside Africa?

Yes, the East African Passport is recognized internationally, though visa requirements depend on destination countries.

What's the difference between this and my national passport?

The East African Passport facilitates easier movement within the EAC region while maintaining all functions of your national passport.

Where can I apply if I'm abroad?

You can apply at your country's embassy or consulate in your current location.
