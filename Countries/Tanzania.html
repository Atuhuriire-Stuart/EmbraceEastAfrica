About Tanzania

Tanzania, officially the United Republic of Tanzania, is a country in East Africa with a population of about 61 million people. It is bordered by Kenya and Uganda to the north, Rwanda, Burundi and DR Congo to the west, and Zambia, Malawi and Mozambique to the south.

Tanzania was formed in 1964 through the union of Tanganyika and Zanzibar. The capital is Dodoma, while Dar es Salaam is the largest city and commercial hub. Tanzania has one of Africa's fastest growing economies, with agriculture, mining, and tourism as key sectors.

Tanzania is home to Mount Kilimanjaro (Africa's highest peak), Serengeti National Park, and the Zanzibar Archipelago. It contains over 30% of its land area as protected nature reserves.

Key Ministries

Ministry of Home Affairs

Responsible for internal security, immigration, and public administration.

Ministry of Foreign Affairs

Handles Tanzania's diplomatic relations and East African Community affairs.

Ministry of Health

Oversees national healthcare policy and public health services.

Ministry of Education

Manages education policy and institutions nationwide.

Ministry of Natural Resources & Tourism

Promotes tourism and manages wildlife conservation.

Tourist Attractions

Serengeti National Park

Famous for the annual Great Migration of over 1.5 million wildebeest and 250,000 zebras. UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mount Kilimanjaro

Africa's highest peak at 5,895 meters with snow-capped summit. Popular hiking destination with several routes to the top.

Zanzibar Archipelago

Tropical islands with white sand beaches, historic Stone Town (UNESCO site), and spice plantations.

Did You Know?

  • Tanzania has the highest concentration of wildlife animals per square kilometer in the world.
  • Lake Tanganyika in western Tanzania is the world's second deepest lake (1,470m).
  • Olduvai Gorge in northern Tanzania is one of the most important paleoanthropological sites, with fossils dating back millions of years.
  • Swahili is the national language, with English used in higher education and administration.
